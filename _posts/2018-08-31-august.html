  • Number added to the “ed-tech dead pool”: 1

Investment Trends


August was a very slow month for investment – not a big surprise as it’s the summer holidays for the Northern hemisphere. There was also no giant deal this month in a Chinese startup, something that’s really driven the level of investment up and up this year.

The amount of money raised in August was the least it’s been all year. (The fewest number of deals, I should note, was in June.)

One of the notable investment trends this month: preschool management software. Three companies raised funding to help people run preschools in their homes: Wonderschool, WeeCare, and Procare Software. Investors include some of the “big money” names in Silicon Valley: Omidyar Network, Andreessen Horowitz, Social Capital Warburg Pincus.

There were also several companies claiming their worksheets are “adaptive” that raised money in August. One of them Knewton, which once boasted about its “mind reading robo tutor in the sky,” raised investment yet again, despite a long track record of vaporware. The company has raised some $182.3 million total, confirming that I do not understand any of the logic behind venture capital.

I would also be remiss if I did not note the passing of ChannelOne into the ed-tech dead pool. Good riddance.

Errata


One of the problems of having “ed-tech funding update” on my To Do list for the last day of each month, is that I sometimes run the numbers before all the news is in. Such was the case for July. After I hit “publish” on July’s article, the press release was issued announcing that DreamBox Learning had raised $130 million. So I’ve adjusted the numbers for July accordingly across my spreadsheets (and any data you download will reflect this change too). But I thought it might be worthwhile to post a quick update here on each month’s funding totals:

  • January: $336.8 million
  • February: $180.8 million
  • March: $404 million
  • April: $331.3 million
  • May: $246.2 million
  • June: $584.5 million
  • July: $757.5 million

With August’s totals, that brings to $2.93 billion that has been invested in education companies so far this year – ahead of the total for the whole year in 2016.
